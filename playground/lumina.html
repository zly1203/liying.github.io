Lumina

A gesture-controlled interactive experiment
This project is a small dive into "vibe coding"—an attempt to translate the abstract images in my head into a physical, digital experience using only gestures and light.
[ Photo: A book on ancient murals or a sketch ]

The Spark

My mind is often filled with stray images—a blend of books I’ve read or things I’ve imagined while lost in a story. For a long time, these thoughts felt too abstract to ever be real; I simply didn't know how to give them a shape.

But AI has become a tool that lets me outline these ideas in a simple way, allowing me to test new ways to interact. When I came across a project using remote hand-tracking, it clicked. I could combine my interest in ancient murals with the simple fun of those "first three words you see" fortune games. I imagined a user in a silent, dark chamber, using their finger as a flickering candle to uncover "destiny" hidden on a weathered wall.

The Process

I started with no background in frontend development. I used Gemini AI Studio to architect the core logic and Cursor to refine the details. It was a fascinating exercise in communication: the hardest part wasn't the logic itself, but finding the right words to describe a sensation.

Trying to explain the "warmth" of a flicker or the "friction" of a hand movement taught me a lot about how we translate human feelings into technical parameters. Every adjustment to the code was really an adjustment to the vibe.

const flicker = Math.sin(time * 8) * 0.15 
              + Math.random() * 0.1;

pointLight.intensity = 18 + flicker * 4;
spotLight.intensity = 50 + flicker * 15;

The Logic of Light

One of the most surprising moments was realizing that the flickering candlelight effect was actually just a mathematical function. I didn't expect that something so natural and poetic could be governed by a Sine wave mixed with a bit of randomness.

Seeing a dry equation manifest as warm, sensory feedback changed my perspective. It made me realize how much of the natural world can be expressed through the elegance of logic, and how much territory is still left to explore at the intersection of math and atmosphere.

Lab Notes

Lumina is a small experiment I continue to tinker with. Here are a few areas I'm still refining:

  • Smoothing Adjusting the hand-tracking data to ensure the light feels responsive without being jittery.
  • Atmosphere Calibrating the light falloff to keep the keywords mysterious.
  • Randomness Ensuring the algorithmic word-selection feels organic and never repetitive.
  • Detection Improving the camera's edge-sensing for different lighting environments.

I see AI as a paintbrush for the imagination. It helps us construct small, meaningful corners of a digital world that used to only exist in our heads.
